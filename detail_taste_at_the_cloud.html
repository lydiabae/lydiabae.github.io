Taste at the cloud

Taste At The Cloud was a signature attraction and food showcase held that took place at Auckland’s waterfront during the excitement of the Rugby World Cup. The event was organised by New Zealand Trade and Enterprise (NZTE) to promote the best of New Zealand food products and cuisine to corporate travellers, buyers and tourists. With an urgent 4 week deadline for the website to go live, our team worked up against the clock to deliver a desktop and mobile experience for one of the largest food events of the year.

Visit

The background

The website was to highlight the event, promote suppliers in both the NZ food and beverage industry, and also to entice people to experience for themselves. The website is built upon rich regional photography and tantalising dishes, accompanied by compelling content, interesting facts, chef biographies and specialist recipes.

Mood board & UI patterns

Each sector contained different background images, fading in and out as users moved from one page to another. For the Gallery section, I used a grid layout (Masonry) to display interesting event images without creating space around the images.

Strategy & Concept

As the the website contained a lot of information, instead of displaying all this information at once, I moved some content to tabs to show and reveal them. This would allow users to find and read content easily without becoming overwhelmed by too much content. For the sector detail templates and recipe detail page, I designed an editorial layout. This would make content more readable and easier to digest for users who are more familiar with print materials.

Not only did the site compliment the event's printed collateral extremely well, it also acted as a source of extra information for visitors to find more information on particular suppliers that were present at the event.

Using the Umbraco content management system the site became a real-time view-finder of the exhibitions launch party, on-going festivities and not to forget a mobile experience which focused on providing time saving directions to the event, opening hours and ticket costs.
